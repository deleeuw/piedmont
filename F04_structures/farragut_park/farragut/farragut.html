Structures: Farragut Park

Jan de Leeuw

Version 06-27-2018

The land for Farragut Park was part of the Lewis Love Donation Land Claim, more precisely from the portion inherited by the reverend Green C. Love. I have not traced yet when the Love family sold it, but I am sure that will not be difficult to find. I also have not find the ordinances and appropriations of the City of Portland to acquire the land and make it a park. Again, those will no doubt turn up eventually.

Note to Jan: Something about Zimmerman-Wells-Brown

I do know that on July 9, 1940 the City of Portland bought the land from the Zimmerman-Wells-Brown Company for $ 10,000. Here is a link to a copy of the deed.

https://drive.google.com/open?id=1C5xvjsPXF0nB-IMLXRU15F4Aev0fAeIe

Note the precise description of the land, which is obviously not a rectangular parcel.  Also note the exclusion of the approximately fifty by two hundred fifty feet parcel deeded to the Portland General Electric Company (I will find out when and by whom later). Portland Maps shows this strip of .24 acres

is still not part of the park, although now it is owned by the Union Pacific Railroad Company. Again, I have not found yet when the ownership was transferred.

 

Here is another map from the County Assessor that shows two other aspects. None of the park is in the Lovewood subdivision, but the strip along Kerby is in Swinton.

 

The northwest corner (here highlighted) is not owned or managed by the Portland Parks department, but by the Portland Property Management department. There used to be a little house in that corner (to be researched: who lived there, when was it built/demolished) that is still visible in a 1950 aerial photo (I think).

The City Archives have many pictures of Farragut Park, beginning with the 1946 clearing.

Eventually I will include some of the more interesting pictures, but keep in mind that the Archives charge ten dollars for each picture, so I am going slow on buying them.

There are also plans and ordinances in the Archives to build and improve the various structures in the park, such as the grandstand, the concession stand, bleachers, baseball field lighting, and so on.

Grandstand seating, concessions stand, comfort station, sports facilities and playground bleachers, scoreboard, and parking. Roland B Hall, designer.

This will be all be added in due time.

It seems that Walter F. Rudd had a concession at Farragut from at least 1959 to 1974.  I will collect some information on him and his activities. From the Oregonian of February 7, 1954.

From the Oregonian of February 2, 1978.

The Archives also contain permits for softball tournaments and associated concessions. To be worked out.

Authorizing an agreement with the Portland Metropolitan Softball Association for the exclusive privilege of operating all concessions at the Erv Lind Stadium at Normandale Park and Farragut Stadium at Farragut Park for a period commencing April 1, 1972, and terminating March 31, 1974

In general it seems that between 1950 and 1980 there was more activity going on at Farragut Park, and the City was investing more resources, than we see these days.
